# Accessible Content Guidelines

We are working to make our content more accessible. This includes not just writing text and making it available to a wide audience on different platforms, but also organizing and categorizing it well so users can find their way through the content easily.

Ask yourself some of these questions while writing:

-   Can someone scan this content quickly and go from heading to heading and know what’s being explained?
-   Are the paragraphs short and scannable?
-   Is the document structured well and broken down in small pieces? Does the information hierarchy make sense?
-   If someone can't see the images included on the page, can they still understand what is being talked about?
-   Does the content work well on a mobile device too?

#### Write with screen readers in mind

Some of our users can’t see. They use screen readers when using our product.

-   **Use descriptive alt text for images**
-   If the text is meant to be read, don’t put it in an image.
-   **Create text alternatives for charts and graphs**
-   **Be brief**
-   Accommodate screen readers’ brief attention. Write only necessary content while using as few words as possible. Be concise.
-   **Use headings and sub-headings**
-   Screen readers jump from heading to heading and facilitate skimming. Use short headings for the main sections of a document.
-   **Left-align headings and make them stand out**
-   Aligning headings and subheadings on the left margin helps screen readers skim and jump across headings easily. Headings on the left margin are easy to skim. Centered headings are harder to skim.

#### Create accessible audio and video

Some of our users can’t hear. They will want to read text versions of videos or recordings.

-   **Captions**
-   Captions and audio descriptions help to make media content more accessible. It also ensures that your content is compliant with the ADA.
-   **Keep things in sync**
-   Make sure the captions are synchronized with the action on the screen.

#### Consider color and contrast

Some of our users are color blind while others may have low vision and need high contrast between background and text.

-   **Don’t use only color to indicate a status change on your UI**
-   Anything that is indicated by color needs to have a secondary way for it to be distinguished. 
-   **Don’t rely on just a visual check of color contrast**
-   It can help find obvious issues, but people look at color and angles differently and using a contrast tool helps.
