# Naming Elements

#### Purpose

The information in this document synthesizes the best practices that were taught in the UX Collective’s Product Naming workshop on May 11, 2022. The purpose of this governance document is meant to guide UI stakeholders in their decisions to finalize and name product features.

#### Beneficiaries

This information benefits the following team members:

-   Product Managers
-   Writers
-   Engineers

#### Best Practices

Before brainstorming names, it is best to consider a few things first. Any type of product naming, big or small, needs to follow a process and set of considerations. In the UX Collective workshop, the following considerations were recommended:

1.  Determine if the product needs a name based on business or user needs.
2.  Clearly define if needs are based on localization, mental models or research insights.
3.  List the core ideas that need to be communicated.
4.  List the benefits we want the user to feel and experience. Ease, freedom, speed, reward, etc.
5.  Determine who are our users and their traits based on what research we have. Are there certain words they already use to describe this feature?
6.  Consider any rules we have - if any- is there a taxonomy this needs to fit into?
7.  Compile a competitor analysis of competitor names and differences/sameness
8.  Come up with words that are unique and could create a branded moment. Test both non-branded and branded names for comprehension
9.  Associate new name with metaphors and meanings the user understands or uses themselves
10.  Create governance rules for consistency and clarity for new names.

#### Quick pivot questions

The following lists considerations UI stakeholders should take when naming a feature:

1.  Could this new name be offensive?
2.  How does this new name sound in a sentence?
3.  Why do we need a new name?

#### Questions for testing

Ask the following questions when testing or considering a term:

1.  Will users recall the term after 5 seconds?
2.  Know your “why”. Why do I want to name this feature or element? Does this feature even need a new name?
3.  Will this term work in multiple languages or cultural contexts?
4.  What is the feature trying to communicate? Draw on words that will succinctly describe the purpose.
5.  What mood do you want users to feel when they think about this feature?
6.  Who are your users? (Generation, interests, goals, digitally native?)
7.  What terms do users use organically? What words should you avoid?
8.  Do you have a taxonomy or naming system this needs to fit into? Are there branding or voice guidelines to adhere to?
9.  Do you have space and time in the product to teach users about the new term?
10.  Will this term delight or annoy users?
11.  Will the branded term benefit the user or are we motivated by something else?
12.  How does this term “sound” written out in a sentence? Does it work with standard conventional standards?
